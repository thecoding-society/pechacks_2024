Domains

Domains

01

Blockchain & Web 3:

Problem Statement


1. Lack of transparency and accountability in the distribution of humanitarian aid, leading to inefficiencies and the potential for misallocation of resources. Donors have limited visibility into how their contributions are utilized, and beneficiaries may not receive aid in a timely or fair manner

2. Hybrid-Healthcare: Build a blockchain-based system for securely storing and managing electronic health records, ensuring privacy, data integrity, and interoperability between healthcare providers.

02

Artificial Intelligence:

Problem Statement


1. Traditional investment decision-making processes lack the ability to effectively leverage real-time data and machine learning techniques, resulting in suboptimal investment decisions.

2. Hybrid-Social Impact: Design an AI system that can detect and mitigate online harassment, hate speech, or fake news, promoting a safer and more inclusive online environment.

03

Healthcare:

Problem Statement


1. The healthcare industry lacks a unified platform for professionals to connect, collaborate, and share knowledge. This hampers effective communication, learning, and the ability to create comprehensive notes. A dedicated platform is needed to bring healthcare professionals together, fostering collaboration, improving understanding, and enhancing diagnosis and treatment practices.

2. Hybrid-AI: Hospitals face challenges in predicting patient demand accurately, optimizing staffing levels, and efficiently allocating resources, leading to inefficiencies and potential resource shortages. Traditional methods often lack the ability to leverage AI algorithms and predictive analytics to address these issues effectively..

04

Social Impact:

Problem Statement


1. There is a lack of effective platforms that promote civic participation and engagement among communities, hindering communication between citizens and local government entities. This leads to limited transparency and inhibits collaborative decision-making processes.

2. There is a significant amount of food surplus generated by restaurants, grocery stores, and individuals, which often goes to waste while many individuals and families experience food insecurity in communities. The lack of an efficient platform to connect food surplus with local food banks or charitable organizations hinders efforts to reduce food waste and address food insecurity effectively.

05

High School Track

Problem Statement


The problem statement for the high school track will be announced at a later date. Stay tuned for updates on our website and social media channels. We're excited to see how you'll tackle the challenge and come up with innovative solutions!

06

Open Track

Your Own Problem Statement


At PEC HACKS, we understand that everyone has unique interests and areas of expertise. If the predefined domains and problem statements don't align with your interests, don't worry! We encourage you to take up your own problem statement.

By taking up your own problem statement, you have the freedom to dive into any domain, and shape your project according to your vision. Our mentors and experts will be there to support you, provide guidance, and help you navigate the challenges along the way.

FAQs

FAQs

Is this Hackathon an online event or offline?


This is an offline event.

Is there any registeration fee?


No, registrations are completely free of cost.

Can I participate without a team?


No, this hackathon is an team event and can participate in teams of 3 or 4.

Should you stay on campus overnight?


Yes, we request all hackers to stay on-campus during the entire hackthon to get the optimal experience.

What are the facilities arranged by the organizers?


Food, accomodation and awesome swags :) are arranged by us.

Can members from different college and branches form a team?


Yes, absolutely we encourage you guys to be diverse and join teams from various backgrounds and collaborate.

What I should bring for the hackathon?


Bring your Student ID, Laptops, Gadgets or anything else you want to work on.

Will the hackathon has Wi-fi facilities?


Yes absolutely, we'll provide Wi-fi Internet facilities.

Rules

Rules

Team Size: Participants should form teams consisting of four members, complying with any restrictions on team composition set by the organizers


Code of Conduct: Participants must adhere to the hackathon's code of conduct, treating others with respect, professionalism, and inclusivity. Harassment, discrimination, or any form of disrespectful behavior will not be tolerated


Original Work: Participants should create and submit original work during the hackathon. Plagiarism, unauthorized use of copyrighted material, or intellectual property violations are strictly prohibited.


Submission Guidelines: Participants should follow the provided submission guidelines, including the required format, documentation, and any additional materials or demonstrations specified by the organizers


Safety and Security: Participants should ensure the safety and security of their own equipment and personal belongings during the hackathon. The organizers are not responsible for any loss or damage incurred


Judging and Decisions: Participants should respect the decisions of the judging panel and organizers. Any disputes or concerns should be addressed through appropriate channels provided by the hackathon organizers


Respect for Venue and Property: Participants should respect the hackathon venue, its facilities, and property. Any damage caused to the venue or equipment may result in disqualification and liability for repair costs.
